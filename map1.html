Charleston to Bandon Tour Route

Distance: 41 miles, Allow: 2 to 3 hours

Starting at Cape Arago State Park, you have an overlook letting you peer down at the boisterous barking sea lions and bobbing seals at Simpson Reef. Traveling north along the Cape Arago Highway you'll rise high above the Pacific on a rocky headland to the Cape Arago Lighthouse, and then either go south on Seven Devils Road or make a stop in Charleston. The fishing port of Charleston sits at the entrance to the deep-water port of Coos Bay, where fishing fleets and freighters find refuge from the open ocean.

Traveling south on Seven Devils Road you quickly arrive to the South Slough National Estuarine Research Reserve where rich tidal flats are explorable with kayaks and boardwalk trails. Continuing south you'll see low lying cranberry bogs, and just before crossing the Coquille River turn west into Bullards Beach State Park. Here you'll find beautiful beaches and the Coquille River Lighthouse and the town Bandon just across the Coquille River. Backtracking to US-101 you will briefly continue south, take a turn west onto Beach Loop Road, and find the rocky shore of Bandon State Natural Area with its seascape of craggy stacks and surf.
