Cow Creek Tour Route

Distance: 45 miles, Allow: 1 to 2 hours

The tour begins 21 miles south of Roseburg at exit 103, and before traveling west on Riddle Bypass Road is your only place to fuel up. Riddle contains remnants of the town's nickel mine and mine tailings, which was spurred due to the arrival of the railroad. The route then parallels with Cow Creek and the route of the original Oregon and California railroad built in the 1870's where many historic railroad structures still stand. Just after Tunnel Number One is the public Recreational Gold Panning Area where it is estimated that miners found 25 percent of Oregon's take of the gold-rush. The tour route takes you through fir forests, under rugged buttes, and along mountains. In the spring waterfalls and wildflowers put on a show, while autumn turns the hardwoods fiery gold and red. Continue along Cow Creek Road where the tour ends just a few miles beyond Glendale at exit 80 and I-5.
