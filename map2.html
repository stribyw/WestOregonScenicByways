Umpqua River Scenic Byway

Distance: 66 miles, Allow: 3 to 5 hours

Before traveling on the byway and just off of interstate is the town of Oakland and "historic row," a National Historic Landmark. There you'll find antique stores and well-preserved 19-century brick buildings, antique stores, museums, and a glimpse of a once busy railroad stop. The town of Sutherlin is just south, and the last chance for fuel before heading northwest on OR-138. The Umpqua River will meet the byway on its crooked course flowing from the Cascade mountains approximately 12 miles from Sutherlin.

Between Kellogg and Elkton is a loopy and scenic stretch of river that offers some of the West's finest smallmouth bass fishing. Shortly thereafter you'll meet OR-38 and continue west and stumble upon Umpqua Valley's unique topography housing a coastal microclimate, producing a surprisingly diverse mix of high-quality wines. In Elkton are winery tasting rooms showcasing pinot noir, pinot gris, cabernet sauvignon, and merlot along with several restaurants. West of Elkton are great river views and the eventual calming of the white water caused by ocean tides before the byway concludes at US-101 and Reedsport. Just sout on US-101 is Winchester Bay and the spectacular Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area, miles and miles of sand dunes scretching all the way to Coos Bay.
