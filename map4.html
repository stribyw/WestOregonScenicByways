Myrtle Creek-Canyonville Tour Route

Distance: 68 miles, Allow: 3 to 4 hours

The tour begins in Canyonville, at exit 98 off I-5, and leads along the South Umpqua River. Approximately 17 miles into the drive you'll see the Milo Academy Covered Bridge looking southeast (on the right side). The bridge was originally built in 1920, but in 1962 it was rebuilt with a steel deck. Locals missed the wooden housing and added it for aesthetics. After passing through Tiller youâ€™ll find the Tiller Ranger Station that was built in 1930, which is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The historic area covers 12 acres, includes nine structures, including a cupola-style fire lookout that is open to the public. The tour swings northwest through Umpqua National Forest where the tour winds under stands of old-growth Douglas fir. Eventually the forest service road joins CR-18, which follows South Myrtle Creek through hills dotted with cattle and sheep. The route ends in Myrtle Creek, where Millsite Park has re-created the 1930 Horse Creek Covered Bridge from Lane County.
