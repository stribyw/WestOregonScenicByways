McKenzie Pass-Santiam Pass
Scenic Byway

Distance: 82 miles, Allow: 4 to 6 hours

Start the loop heading northwest on US-20/OR-126 from Sisters, named for the Three Sisters - 10,000-foot volcanic peaks rising up to the southwest. Near the 6,436-foot cinder cone of Black Butte there's a side road that leads north to Camp Sherman. Continuing on the byway you’ll get a look at Mt. Washington, and as the road climbs towards the Santiam Pass the road skirts by glacial-carved Suttle Lake. At the 4,817-foot Santiam Pass, you’ll cross The Pacific Crest National Scenic Trail, and before turning south on OR-126 there will be a turn off for the mysterious Lost Lake.

While driving on OR-126, the McKenzie River Highway, you’ll be following the Upper McKenzie River and the McKenzie River National Recreational Trail. Mountain bikers and hikers have 26 miles of trail that showcases the beautiful peacock-blue waters. Fly-fishers hold the McKenzie in high regard, casting for red-band and rainbow trout. The McKenzie drift boat originated here and many area guides work the McKenzie rapids in one of these time-tested boats. Or take a canoe out for a paddle on Clear Lake, fed by numerous springs and formed when an ancient lava flow dammed a stream. Sahalie Falls and Koosah Falls are viewable with quick hikes, and then there’s a 2.6 mile hike to Tamolic Pool, or sometimes listed as “the Blue Pool.” Here the river reappears iridescent blue from a 3-mile underground detour through a lava tube.

Past Belknap Springs the byway turns east onto OR-242, the McKenzie River Highway. The byway then does switchbacks through an area known as Deadhorse Grade, climbing nearly 1,200 feet in just 4 miles. Views of North Sister and Middle Sister will come at the top of this ascent, where the forest abruptly is replaced by an expanse of dark and broken lava that stretches for 65 square miles. The Dee Wright Observatory, with stunning views of many Cascade peaks, will be your last stop before reentering the forest and returning to Sisters.
